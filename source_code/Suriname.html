Suriname - Country in South America

Suriname is a small country on the northeastern coast of South America. It's defined by vast swaths of tropical rainforest, Dutch colonial architecture and a melting-pot culture. On its Atlantic coast is the capital, Paramaribo, where palm gardens grow near Fort Zeelandia, a 17th-century trading post. Paramaribo is also home to Saint Peter and Paul Basilica, a towering wood cathedral consecrated in 1885. Wikipedia
Capital: Paramaribo
Currency: Surinamese dollar
Official script: Dutch
Population: 558,368 (2016) World Bank
